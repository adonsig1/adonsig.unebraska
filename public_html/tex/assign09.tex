\question{
Suppose that $\zero=(0,0)$, $r > 0$ and $f=(f_1,f_2)$
mapping $B_r(\zero)$ into $\bR^2$ is $C^1$.
Prove that if $df_\zero$ is invertible and $f^{-1}=(g_1,g_2)$ is the
local inverse, then
\[ D_1(g_1)(f(\zero)) = \frac{ D_2(f_2)(\zero)}{Jf_\zero}, \qquad
   D_2(g_1)(f(\zero)) = \frac{ -D_2(f_1)(\zero)}{Jf_\zero},
\]
and
\[ D_1(g_2)(f(\zero)) = \frac{ -D_1(f_2)(\zero)}{Jf_\zero}, \qquad
   D_2(g_2)(f(\zero)) = \frac{ D_1(f_1)(\zero)}{Jf_\zero},
\]
where $J f_\zero$ is the determinant of the $2\times 2$ matrix for
$df_\zero$.
}{
